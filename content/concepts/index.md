# Concepts

The key ideas — philosophical, psychological, and poetic — that structure the Relational Frontier's intellectual project.

## Cornerstone Concepts

These are the highest-gravity nodes in the garden — the ideas that almost everything else connects back to:

- [[resonance|Resonance]] — Hartmut Rosa's framework for aliveness vs. alienation
- [[i-thou|I-Thou]] — Martin Buber's distinction between genuine encounter and objectification
- [[imaginal|The Imaginal]] — Henry Corbin's realm between intellect and matter
- [[meaning-crisis|Meaning Crisis]] — John Vervaeke's diagnosis of modernity
- [[intersubjectivity|Intersubjectivity]] — the mutual constitution of self through relation
- [[relational-ground|Relational Ground]] — relationality as ontological, not secondary

## Existential & Psychological

- [[alienation|Alienation]] — The world as mute and unresponsive; Rosa's counterpart to resonance
- [[true-self-false-self|True Self / False Self]] — Winnicott's distinction between aliveness and compliance
- [[holding-environment|Holding Environment]] — Winnicott's concept for the relational container of growth
- [[containment|Containment]] — Bion's concept for the metabolic transformation of unbearable experience
- [[transitional-space|Transitional Space]] — Winnicott's intermediate realm of play, culture, and creativity
- [[transitional-objects|Transitional Objects]] — The first symbolic bridges between inner and outer reality
- [[mutual-recognition|Mutual Recognition]] — Benjamin's concept of genuinely seeing and being seen
- [[the-daimonic|The Daimonic]] — May's term for the raw energy of aliveness and creativity
- [[the-face-of-the-other|The Face of the Other]] — Levinas's concept of the ethical summons in the other's presence

## Philosophical & Ontological

- [[being-in-the-world|Being-in-the-World]] — Heidegger's account of human existence as always already situated
- [[active-imagination|Active Imagination]] — Jung's method for direct dialogue with the unconscious
