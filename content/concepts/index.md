# Concepts

The key ideas — philosophical, psychological, and poetic — that structure the Relational Frontier's intellectual project.

## Diagnosing Modernity

- [[meaning-crisis|Meaning Crisis]] — John Vervaeke's diagnosis of modernity's loss of participatory knowing and sacred connection
- [[alienation|Alienation]] — The world as mute and unresponsive; Rosa's counterpart to resonance
- [[resonance|Resonance]] — Hartmut Rosa's framework for aliveness vs. alienation

## The Ground of Relation

- [[i-thou|I-Thou]] — Martin Buber's distinction between genuine encounter and objectification
- [[intersubjectivity|Intersubjectivity]] — The mutual constitution of self through relation
- [[relational-ground|Relational Ground]] — Relationality as ontological, not secondary
- [[the-between|The Between]] — Buber's name for the sphere that arises in genuine encounter
- [[confirmation|Confirmation]] — Buber's concept of making the other present in their uniqueness
- [[mutual-recognition|Mutual Recognition]] — Benjamin's concept of genuinely seeing and being seen
- [[the-third|The Third]] — The intersubjective space created between two people; autonomous and structuring

## Realms of Experience

- [[imaginal|The Imaginal]] — Henry Corbin's realm between intellect and matter
- [[mundus-imaginalis|Mundus Imaginalis]] — The intermediate world where images have ontological reality
- [[transitional-space|Transitional Space]] — Winnicott's intermediate realm of play, culture, and creativity
- [[transitional-objects|Transitional Objects]] — The first symbolic bridges between inner and outer reality

## Becoming & Transformation

- [[individuation|Individuation]] — Jung's process of becoming who you uniquely are
- [[the-self|The Self]] — Jung's concept of the integrated totality of the psyche
- [[true-self-false-self|True Self / False Self]] — Winnicott's distinction between aliveness and compliance
- [[gnosis|Gnosis]] — Direct, participatory knowing vs. propositional knowledge
- [[relevance-realization|Relevance Realization]] — Vervaeke's framework for how we zero in on what matters
- [[four-ps|The Four Ps]] — Vervaeke's framework for the four orders of knowing: Participatory, Perspectival, Procedural, Propositional

## The Ethical & the Other

- [[the-face-of-the-other|The Face of the Other]] — Levinas's concept of the ethical summons in the other's presence

## Care & Containment

- [[holding-environment|Holding Environment]] — Winnicott's concept for the relational container of growth
- [[containment|Containment]] — Bion's concept for the metabolic transformation of unbearable experience
- [[good-enough-mother|Good-Enough Mother]] — Winnicott's concept of care that is sufficient without being perfect
- [[enactment|Enactment]] — When unconscious patterns are lived out in the therapeutic relationship

## Existential Structures

- [[being-in-the-world|Being-in-the-World]] — Heidegger's account of human existence as always already situated
- [[dasein|Dasein]] — Heidegger's term for the kind of being that we are
- [[care|Care (Sorge)]] — Heidegger's concept of the fundamental structure of human existence
- [[thrownness|Thrownness]] — Heidegger's concept of our being already situated in a world not of our choosing

## Practices & Methods

- [[active-imagination|Active Imagination]] — Jung's method for direct dialogue with the unconscious
- [[tawil|Ta'wīl]] — Corbin's concept of esoteric hermeneutics; symbolic interpretation that returns to origins

## Visionary & Mystical

- [[imaginal-body|Imaginal Body]] — Corbin's concept of the subtle body that perceives in the imaginal realm
- [[theophany|Theophany]] — Divine self-disclosure; God's manifestation to human awareness
- [[the-daimonic|The Daimonic]] — May's term for the raw energy of aliveness and creativity
- [[the-unthought-known|The Unthought Known]] — Bollas's concept of experience that has been lived but never symbolized
