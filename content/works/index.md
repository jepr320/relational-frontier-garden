# Works

The foundational texts — books, lecture series, and major essays — that shape the Relational Frontier's intellectual landscape.

## Relational Psychoanalysis

- [[playing-and-reality|Playing and Reality]] — D.W. Winnicott's exploration of transitional space, creativity, and the intermediate realm
- [[what-alive-means|What Alive Means]] — Thomas Ogden on the analytic third and what it means to be genuinely alive
- [[the-shadow-of-the-object|The Shadow of the Object]] — Christopher Bollas on the unthought known and the transformational object

## Phenomenology & Existentialism

- [[being-and-time|Being and Time]] — Martin Heidegger's foundational work on Dasein, care, and temporality
- [[i-and-thou|I and Thou]] — Martin Buber's philosophy of genuine encounter and the between
- [[the-courage-to-be|The Courage to Be]] — Paul Tillich on faith, courage, and the confrontation with non-being
- [[the-denial-of-death|The Denial of Death]] — Ernest Becker on the human condition and the denial of mortality

- [[the-world-turned-inside-out|The World Turned Inside Out]] — Tom Cheetham on Corbin's method and vision
- [[the-art-of-memory|The Art of Memory]] — Frances Yates on Renaissance memory techniques and hermetic tradition

- [[memories-dreams-reflections|Memories, Dreams, Reflections]] — Carl Jung's autobiography and spiritual journey
- [[re-visioning-psychology|Re-Visioning Psychology]] — James Hillman on soul-making and archetypal imagination
